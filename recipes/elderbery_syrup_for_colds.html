Elderbery syrup for colds

Recipe Page


Go To Home Page


Gather berry clusters after the first hard frost. Remove stems and place berries in a large pan. Heat slowly, mashing the berries. Pour the juice and berries into a clean pillowcase and hang above a lare pot and let it drip/drain overnight (you can skip this part if using a juicer). The next morning, measure the juice and add an equal amount of honey. Simmer over medium-high heat until thick and of a syrup-like consistency. Pour into sterilized bottles and seal in a boiling water bath for twenty minutes. Use as a cold preventative.
