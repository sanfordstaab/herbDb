Elderberry oatmeal soap for dry, scaly skin

Recipe Page


Go To Home Page

Ingredients:
  • 12 oz. vegetable shortening
  • 2 oz lye
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup dried, crushed elderberry leaves
  • 1 oz. essential oil (I like chamomile, lavender or mint)
  • Soap molds (optional)
  • Rubber gloves
  • Safety glasses
  • Apron
Put on rubber gloves, safety glasses and apron if you have them, because of the lye.

Mix the water and lye in a stainless steel or high-temp glass container (this is going to get HOT - up to 200° F), stirring carefully until the lye is dissolved.

Always add lye to water - NEVER the other way around!

Do This in a well-veltilated area.

Add the shortening to the lye mix and allow to melt.
When comnpletely melted, stir in the elderberry leaves and oats.
Continue stirring until the mixture resembles pudding.
Add the essential oil of your choice.
Pour into soap molds if you have them.
24-48 hours later, unmold or cut into bars.
The sopap needs to age for at least three weeks before wrapping and using.
6 weeks worked for us.
