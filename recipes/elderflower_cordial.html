Elderflower cordial

Go To Home Page


Ingredients:
  • 30 heads of elder flower
  • 3 lbs sugar
  • 4 pints water
  • 2 oz. citric acid
  • one chopped lemon
  • one chopped orange
  • jelly bag and bottle
Pick thirty heads of elderflower on a dry summer day.
Choose only those that smell lemony and fresh. (When it's damp weather or where the flowers are growing in the shade, the flowers can have an unpleasant smell.)
Boil two pounds of sugar in four points of water in a large saucepan for about five minutes.
Pour this into a large ceramic bowl and add two oz. citirc acid, a chopped lemon and a chopped orange.
Add the elderflower heads and stir well.
Cover with a clean clost and leave for four days, stirring every day.
Strain through a jelly bag and bottle.
For long term soorage, the cordial cna be fronzen.
To dring, dilute to taste with cool sparkling water.
This can also be made with hot water to encourage sweating for colds and fevers.
