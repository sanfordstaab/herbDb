Ingredients:
  • Jojoba oil
  • Elder flower oil (made 3 weeks prior!)
  • 1/2 oz glycerin
  • 2 oz. fresh elederflowers
  • 1 Tblspn infused elderflower oil
  • 1/2 oz. alcohol-free Witch Hazel
  • 1 Tblspn Honey
On a sunny spring morning after the dew ahas gone, gather some of the blooming flowers and shake the blossoms into a bag. Pack the flowers in a jar and cover with jojoba oil. Allow the flowers to infuse in a warm place for three weeks. Strain the oil (coffee filter works best)
(The unused oiil will last for a couple of years if stored in sealed jars or bottles.)
To make the cream: whisk together the glycerin, Witch Hazel, elderflower, water and honey until well mixed. Keep whisking and slowly add the elderflower oil in a steady stream. Spoon the resulting mixture/cream into small jars with tihgt fitting lists and stor in the refrigerator. This cream is best when use within two months.
